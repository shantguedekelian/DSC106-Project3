A rationale for your design decisions. How did you choose your particular visual encodings and interaction techniques? What alternatives did you consider and how did you arrive at your ultimate choices?

  • Visual Encodings: We chose to represent each fire with a red circle because Red is often associated with fire and danger, making it an intuitive choice for representing fire incidents. The size of the circles is adjusted based on the intensity of the fire detection, allowing users to quickly assess the seriousness of each fire.
  • Interaction Techniques: We used zooming to allow users to explore different regions of the map in detail. This is particularly useful for examining areas with a high density of fire incidents. The time slider enables users to filter fire incidents based on the hour of the day, providing insights into temporal patterns of fire occurrences. The play button animates the time slider, allowing users to observe how fire incidents change over time without manual input. Lastly we added a tooltip which provides additional information about each fire when hovered over, enhancing the user experience by providing context-specific details.
  • Alternatives Considered: We considered using color gradients to represent fire intensity, but decided against it to maintain clarity and avoid potential confusion with other map elements. We also thought about using heatmaps to represent fire density, but opted for individual circles to provide more granular information about each fire incident.
